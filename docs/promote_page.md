# Promise / Value Proposition Template

## 1. Core Promise (WIIFM)
- **Primary Value Proposition:**  
  State in one sentence what customers get and why it matters to them (their WIIFM).  
  *Example: “We automatically expose hidden security flaws before attackers do — without changing your workflow.”*

- **Customer Outcome in One Line:**  
  Describe the transformation or result your product creates.  
  *Example: “From reactive firefighting to predictable, automated application security.”*

---

## 2. Customer Job & Context
- **Customer Job-to-be-Done:**  
  Summarize the key job your user is trying to accomplish.  
  *Example: “Ship features fast without compromising security.”*

- **Current Frustration / Risk:**  
  Describe the main risk, friction, or hassle they experience today.  
  *Example: “Security checks are slow, manual, and always late in the release cycle.”*

---

## 3. Tangible Benefits
- **Functional Benefits (What it does):**  
  List the top 3–5 concrete benefits.  
  *Example: “Automated code scanning, instant risk visibility, zero-config setup.”*

- **Emotional / Strategic Benefits (How it feels / why it matters):**  
  Describe how life improves for them beyond features.  
  *Example: “Peace of mind, fewer fire drills, more focus on innovation.”*

---

## 4. Differentiation & Proof
- **Why We’re Different:**  
  Explain in 1–2 sentences what makes this solution uniquely valuable.  
  *Example: “Unlike traditional scanners, we run continuously in your environment with zero manual tuning.”*

- **Credibility Signals / Proof Points:**  
  Add specific signals that back up the promise.  
  *Example: “Detected 30% more critical issues in pilot tests; used by 5+ high-growth SaaS teams.”*

---

## 5. Offer & Model
- **Core Offer Structure:**  
  Clarify what they actually get (product, plan, or bundle).  
  *Example: “Self-serve security scanner with unlimited repos for small teams.”*

- **Risk-Reversal / Commitment Level:**  
  Reduce friction with trial, guarantee, or no-commit hook.  
  *Example: “Free, fully-automated scanning for your first repo — no credit card required.”*

---

## 6. Key Messaging Lineup
- **Hero Headline:**  
  One bold line that could sit on the top of your website or deck.  
  *Example: “Trust nothing. Scan everything.”*

- **Supporting Sub-Headline:**  
  Clarify the hero line with a descriptive follow-up.  
  *Example: “Continuous, zero-touch application security for teams that ship fast.”*

- **1–3 WIIFM Bullets:**  
  Short, sharp bullets written from the customer’s perspective.  
  *Example: “I catch issues early, I protect my brand, I move faster with less risk.”*

---

## 7. Promise Summary Statement
- **One-Paragraph Summary:**  
  Combine customer, job, pains, and gains into a concise promise narrative.  
  *Example: “For DevSecOps teams who are tired of slow, noisy scanners, [Product] delivers continuous, automated security checks that fit directly into your existing workflow. You ship faster, reduce risk, and stay ahead of attackers — without extra overhead or manual tuning.”*

---

> **Prompt Use Note:**  
> Use this template to define a sharp, customer-centric promise before generating your business plan or pitch deck. Each section should include 1–3 clear sentences focused on WIIFM (“What’s in it for me?”), measurable outcomes, and why your solution is the obvious choice.

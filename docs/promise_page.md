# Promise / Value Proposition Template

## 1. Core Promise
- **Value Proposition Statement:**  
  Define the core promise your brand or product makes to customers — the “Why Us?”  
  *Example: We give small businesses effortless tools to grow loyal customer relationships without extra work.*

---

## 2. Customer Benefit (WIIFM – What’s In It For Me)
- **Functional Benefits:**  
  Describe what the product enables or improves.  
  *Example: Automates daily tasks so users can focus on higher-value work.*

- **Emotional Benefits:**  
  Explain how it makes customers feel or the confidence it delivers.  
  *Example: Peace of mind, empowerment, pride in professional results.*

- **Financial / Practical Gains:**  
  Quantify tangible outcomes if possible.  
  *Example: Saves customers up to 10 hours per week and reduces operating costs by 20%.*

---

## 3. Differentiation
- **Unique Advantage / Edge:**  
  Identify what makes this solution distinct in the market.  
  *Example: Combines automation with personalized service for a seamless experience competitors can’t match.*

- **Proof Points:**  
  Include supporting evidence or early validation.  
  *Example: Backed by 1,000 beta users and recognized by major industry partners.*

---

## 4. Brand Promise Summary
- **Concise Positioning Statement:**  
  Craft a single line integrating who you serve, what you deliver, and why it matters.  
  *Example: “For time-strapped professionals, our platform transforms complexity into control — enabling smarter growth with less effort.”*

- **Customer Transformation:**  
  Describe the “before and after” state of your users.  
  *Example: From overwhelmed and reactive → to confident and in control.*

---

## 5. Strategic Relevance
- **Business Impact Connection:**  
  Link the promise directly to your company’s mission or market opportunity.  
  *Example: This promise drives brand loyalty and customer retention, positioning us for sustainable growth.*


## 4. Existing Solutions & Gaps (PROMISE PAGE)
- **Current Alternatives:**  
  What do users currently use (direct or indirect fixes)?  
  *Example: Manual spreadsheets, off-the-shelf tools like Calendly.*

- **Limitations or Frustrations with Current Solutions:**  
  Highlight why these are insufficient.  
  *Example: Lack of integration with payment platforms; poor client retention features.*


---

> **Prompt Use Note:**  
> Use this template to define the customer-facing promise clearly before generating a business plan. Each section should contain 1–3 clear, compelling sentences showing both emotional resonance and measurable business value.
